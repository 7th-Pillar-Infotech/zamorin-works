§02 Approach · Home

A predictable rhythm: Discover. Architect. Deliver.

Every engagement follows the same three phases. You always know which phase you're in, what's being decided this week, and what triggers the next.

01

Discover.

Weeks 1–2 · Fixed fee

Stakeholder interviews, current-state assessment, and a written brief that names success criteria, constraints, and known risks. You walk away with a clear document — whether or not we work together further.

02

Architect.

Weeks 2–6 · Fixed fee

Solution design, delivery plan, named team, and commercials. You approve the plan in writing before any build work begins. No "we'll figure it out in sprint 3."

03

Deliver.

Engagement-dependent

Build, test, ship, and stay through stabilization. Weekly written status. One accountable principal. Handover only when you sign off — not when the contract clock runs out.

04

Steady-state.

Optional retainer

If you want us to keep supporting what we built, we move into a Managed IT retainer with quarterly reviews to right-size the commitment. Easy to scale up, easy to off-board.

§ Operating habits
Weekly cadence

Habits that show up every week.

The visible artifacts of project discipline. Inherited from twenty years of Zamorin Group delivery.

  • Written status every Friday. What shipped, what's blocked, what's coming. Same template every week, archived for the duration of the engagement.
  • One accountable principal per engagement. Not a "team." A named person, with their direct number.
  • Decision log, not meeting minutes. Every consequential decision is logged with date, owner, and rationale — so we can reconstruct why six months later.
  • Pre-mortems on day one. Before kicking off, we name the three most likely ways the project fails. Then we plan against them.
  • Commercials match risk. Fixed scope where the brief is clear; time-and-materials where it isn't. Never both at once, never hidden margin.
